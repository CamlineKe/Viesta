## Normal Blood Pressure Levels (A General Reference)

Blood pressure is measured in millimeters of mercury (mmHg) and expressed as two numbers.

### Normal BP Range
- **Systolic:** < 120 mmHg
- **Diastolic:** < 80 mmHg

This is commonly written as "below 120/80."

### Elevated BP (Not Yet Hypertension)
- **Systolic:** 120–129 mmHg
- **Diastolic:** < 80 mmHg

### Hypertension (High BP)
- **Stage 1:** 130–139 / 80–89 mmHg
- **Stage 2:** ≥ 140 / ≥ 90 mmHg

These ranges are based on widely accepted international guidelines.

## Normal Blood Sugar Levels (General Reference)

Blood sugar (glucose) levels vary depending on whether a person has fasted or not.

### Fasting Blood Sugar (8–12 hours without food)
- **Normal:** 70–99 mg/dL
- **Prediabetes:** 100–125 mg/dL
- **Diabetes:** ≥ 126 mg/dL (confirmed by a healthcare provider)

### Random / Non-Fasting Blood Sugar
- **Normal:** < 140 mg/dL
- **Prediabetes:** 140–199 mg/dL
- **Diabetes:** ≥ 200 mg/dL (with symptoms, confirmed by a clinician)

### HbA1c (3-month average blood sugar)
- **Normal:** < 5.7%
- **Prediabetes:** 5.7–6.4%
- **Diabetes:** ≥ 6.5% (confirmed by a clinician)

## Important Notes

- These values are general population references, not personalized targets.
- Anyone with abnormal readings should consult a qualified healthcare professional for evaluation and guidance.
- Lifestyle factors — diet, exercise, stress, sleep — strongly influence both BP and blood sugar.
- Herbal supplements (like Bio1 Gluco or Bio1 Sterol) may support wellness routines but do not replace medical treatment.
